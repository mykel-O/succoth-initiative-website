What We Do

Programs & Services

Comprehensive support covering every stage of your housing journey — from pre-arrival to long-term stability.

🏡

Housing Search Support

Personalized help finding safe, affordable rental housing — including connections to landlords who welcome newcomers and immigrants.

⚖️

Tenant Rights & Advocacy

Education on your legal rights as a tenant in Canada, guidance during disputes, and advocacy against discrimination and wrongful eviction.

📚

Workshops & Education

Free in-person and virtual workshops on renting in Canada, understanding leases, building credit, and moving toward homeownership.

💬

One-on-One Counselling

Confidential housing counselling sessions with experienced advisors who understand the unique challenges facing Black and immigrant families.

🏦

Financial Literacy

Build your credit history, open a bank account, and plan for homeownership — all the financial foundations for housing stability.

🌐

Settlement Connections

Referrals to community organizations, government services, and settlement agencies to support your full transition into Canadian life.

Our Mission

Built by the Community, for the Community

Succoth Initiative Inc. was founded on the belief that every family deserves a safe place to call home — regardless of where they came from.

  • Education and resources tailored to Black and immigrant lived experiences
  • Advocacy that challenges systemic barriers in Canada's housing market
  • A network of trusted landlords, legal advisors, and settlement workers
  • A community-centred approach rooted in dignity and respect

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most often from newcomers and their families.

Yes — 100% of our services are completely free for individuals and families. We are a non-profit organization funded by grants, donations, and community partners. You will never be charged for any support, workshop, counselling session, or resource.
No. Our services are open to all newcomers, immigrants, permanent residents, refugees, and internationally protected persons. We serve anyone who has recently arrived in Canada and needs housing support, regardless of immigration status.
No. Under the Canadian Human Rights Act and provincial human rights codes, landlords cannot discriminate based on race, national or ethnic origin, colour, religion, age, sex, or family status. If you have experienced discrimination, contact us — we can help you understand your options and file a complaint.
Yes, though it can be challenging. We work with a network of landlords who are open to renting to newcomers without Canadian credit history. We also provide guidance on how to present your financial situation (e.g., employment letters, international credit reports, larger deposits) to strengthen your rental application.
We aim to respond to all requests within 1 business day. For urgent housing situations — such as risk of homelessness or illegal eviction — please call us directly and we will prioritize your case. Our team includes advisors who speak multiple languages.
Yes. We currently offer services in English, French, Amharic, Somali, Tagalog, Arabic, and Haitian Creole. Let us know your preferred language when you get in touch and we will match you with an appropriate advisor.
